Chocolate Chip Cookies

Warm, sweet, gooey, and full of memories.

See Recipe

Why I Love Them

Chocolate chip cookies matter to me because my grandmother taught me how to make them when I was eight. The smell of vanilla and melted chocolate feels like family holidays and calm nights after homework. They're simple, warm, and make everything feel a little better.

Quick Facts

  • Invented in the 1930s in Massachusetts by Ruth Wakefield.
  • Best texture: crispy edges + soft, gooey center.
  • Add a pinch of sea salt to make the chocolate pop!

Simple Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup butter
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cream butter + sugars. Add eggs + vanilla.
  3. Mix in dry ingredients, then chocolate chips.
  4. Scoop dough, bake 9-12 minutes.
  5. Let cool and enjoy warm!

Why Others Might Love Them

They’re nostalgic, easy to bake, and perfect for sharing. You can add nuts, caramel, sprinkles, or change the chocolate flavor. Almost everyone has a cookie memory.
